# Tool Opportunities: Charitable Giving Strategies

## Overview

Charitable giving is one of the most powerful tax tools available to high-income earners, yet most don't optimize it. Beyond the feel-good aspect, strategic giving can provide immediate tax deductions, avoid capital gains, and enable wealth transferâ€”all while supporting causes you care about.

## The Problem

### Missed Opportunities
- Donating cash when appreciated stock is available
- Not bunching donations in high-income years
- Giving directly when DAF would be better
- Ignoring Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs) after 70.5

### 2025-2026 Tax Changes
The One Big Beautiful Bill Act brings significant changes:
- **New 0.5% AGI floor** on charitable deductions (starting 2026)
- **35% cap on deduction value** for top earners (down from 37%)
- 2025 is the last year under more favorable rules
- Strategic giving in 2025 captures better tax treatment

### Complexity
- Different rules for cash vs appreciated assets
- DAF vs direct giving tradeoffs
- CRT structuring complexity
- AGI percentage limits vary by asset type

## Key Strategies

### 1. Donate Appreciated Stock (Not Cash)
- **Deduct full fair market value** (not cost basis)
- **Avoid capital gains tax** on appreciation
- **Double benefit:** Deduction + capital gains avoidance
- Must have held >1 year for full benefit

**Example:** Stock bought at $10K, now worth $100K
- Sell and donate cash: Pay ~$18K capital gains tax, donate $82K
- Donate stock directly: Donate $100K, deduct $100K, no capital gains

### 2. Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs)
- Contribute now, get immediate deduction
- Distribute to charities over time
- Investment growth is tax-free
- "Bunching" strategy enabler

### 3. Bunching Strategy
- Combine multiple years' donations into one year
- Exceed standard deduction threshold
- Take standard deduction in other years
- Especially powerful after TCJA increased standard deduction

### 4. Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)
- Available after age 70.5
- Donate directly from IRA to charity
- Up to $105,000/year (2024)
- Counts toward RMD but not taxable income
- Better than taking RMD and donating

### 5. Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs)
- Donate appreciated assets to trust
- Receive income stream for life/term
- Remainder goes to charity
- Immediate partial deduction
- Capital gains deferral

## Tool Opportunities

### 1. Appreciated Asset Donation Calculator

**Problem:** People don't realize how much more valuable donating appreciated stock is versus cash.

**Features:**
- Input: Cost basis, current value, holding period
- Output: Tax savings comparison (cash vs stock)
- Capital gains avoided calculation
- Effective "cost" of donation
- Wash sale warning if planning to rebuy

**Key insight:** For highly appreciated assets, the "cost" of a $10K donation might only be $6K after tax benefits.

---

### 2. Donor-Advised Fund Optimizer

**Problem:** People don't understand when/how to use DAFs effectively.

**Features:**
- Bunching strategy calculator
- Standard deduction comparison
- Optimal contribution timing
- Investment growth projector
- Grant planning tool
- Multiple charity tracking

**Use cases:**
- High-income year (IPO, bonus, stock vesting)
- Bunching multiple years
- Receiving inherited appreciated assets
- Before moving to no-income-tax state

---

### 3. Charitable Giving Tax Savings Calculator

**Problem:** People underestimate the tax benefits of strategic giving.

**Features:**
- Input: Income, filing status, gift amount, gift type
- Output: Federal + state tax savings
- Effective "cost" of donation
- AGI limit warnings
- Carryforward projections

**AGI limits:**
- Cash to public charities: 60% of AGI
- Appreciated property: 30% of AGI
- Private foundations: Lower limits
- Carryforward: 5 years

---

### 4. Bunching Strategy Planner

**Problem:** With high standard deduction ($29,200 married in 2024), many miss out on itemizing.

**Features:**
- Multi-year giving projection
- Standard vs itemized comparison each year
- Optimal bunching schedule
- State tax consideration
- DAF funding recommendation

**Example:** Instead of $10K/year (below standard deduction), give $30K every 3 years (exceeds standard deduction in that year).

---

### 5. QCD (Qualified Charitable Distribution) Tool

**Problem:** People over 70.5 don't know about QCDs or how to use them.

**Features:**
- Eligibility checker (age 70.5+)
- RMD coordination
- Tax savings vs regular donation
- Tracking against $105K annual limit
- IRA balance impact projection

**Why QCDs are powerful:**
- Satisfies RMD without taxable income
- Better than deducting (reduces AGI, not just taxable income)
- No AGI limits
- Works even if you don't itemize

---

### 6. Charitable Remainder Trust Analyzer

**Problem:** CRTs are powerful but complex. People don't know if one makes sense.

**Features:**
- Input: Asset details, desired income, time horizon
- Output: Income stream projection
- Partial deduction calculation
- Capital gains avoided
- Comparison to just selling and investing
- Wealth replacement insurance option

**Good candidates for CRT:**
- Highly appreciated, low-yield assets
- Business sale proceeds
- Concentrated stock positions
- Desire for income + charitable impact

---

### 7. Stock Donation vs Sale Comparison

**Problem:** People don't run the numbers on donating stock vs selling.

**Features:**
- Side-by-side comparison
- Different holding periods
- State tax consideration
- Wash sale guidance (if rebuying)
- Recommendation engine

---

### 8. Charitable Giving Impact Tracker

**Problem:** People lose track of their giving and don't see the cumulative impact.

**Features:**
- Track donations over time
- Tax benefit history
- Charity organization tracker
- DAF grant history
- Year-over-year comparison
- Goal setting and progress

---

### 9. 2025 vs 2026 Charitable Giving Analyzer

**Problem:** With 2026 tax changes, 2025 giving is more valuable but people don't know this.

**Features:**
- Current rules vs 2026 rules comparison
- Tax savings difference
- Accelerated giving recommendation
- DAF funding strategy
- Deadline countdown

**Key 2026 changes:**
- 0.5% AGI floor on charitable deductions
- 35% cap on deduction value for top earners
- Bunching becomes more valuable

---

### 10. Private Foundation vs DAF Comparison

**Problem:** Very high net worth individuals may consider private foundations without understanding tradeoffs.

**Features:**
- Side-by-side comparison
- Cost analysis (setup, ongoing)
- Control and flexibility comparison
- Tax deduction differences
- Payout requirements
- Privacy considerations

**DAF advantages:** Lower cost, simpler, immediate full deduction
**Foundation advantages:** More control, family involvement, perpetual

## Tax Deduction Limits Reference

### Cash Donations
- **Public charities:** 60% of AGI
- **Private foundations:** 30% of AGI

### Appreciated Property (held >1 year)
- **Public charities:** 30% of AGI (or 50% at cost basis)
- **Private foundations:** 20% of AGI

### Carryforward
- Unused deductions carry forward 5 years

### 2026 Changes (OBBBA)
- New 0.5% AGI floor before deductions count
- 35% cap on tax benefit for top bracket

## High-Income Charitable Strategies

### $200K-$500K Income
- Donate appreciated stock instead of cash
- Consider bunching with DAF
- Time large gifts with high-income years
- Use QCD if over 70.5

### $500K-$1M Income
- Same as above, plus:
- DAF bunching essential
- Consider CRT for concentrated positions
- Coordinate with RSU vesting/stock sales
- State tax optimization

### $1M+ Income
- All of above, plus:
- Private foundation consideration
- Pooled income funds
- Conservation easements
- Complex CRT structures
- Family charitable planning
