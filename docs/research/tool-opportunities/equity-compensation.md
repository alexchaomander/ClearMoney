# Tool Opportunities: Equity Compensation

## Overview

Equity compensation is one of the most complex and high-stakes areas of personal finance for tech workers. Stock options, RSUs, and ESPPs can represent 50-90% of total compensation, yet most employees don't fully understand them. This creates massive opportunity for educational tools.

## The Problem

### Complexity Gap
- **86% of startup employees' net worth** is in stock options (Secfi data)
- Most HR explanations are inadequate
- Tax implications are counterintuitive
- Wrong decisions can cost tens of thousands of dollars

### Information Asymmetry
- Companies benefit from employees not understanding equity
- Financial advisors often don't specialize in equity comp
- Generic advice doesn't account for individual circumstances
- Mistakes are often irreversible

### High Stakes Decisions
- Exercise timing can swing taxes by 6 figures
- 83(b) elections have 30-day irrevocable deadlines
- Leaving a company triggers 90-day exercise windows
- IPO/acquisition creates concentrated wealth

## Tool Opportunities

### 1. RSU Tax Calculator

**Problem:** RSUs are taxed as ordinary income at vesting, but many employees don't understand how much will be withheld or owe at tax time.

**Features:**
- Input: RSU shares, stock price, vesting date, tax filing status
- Output: Federal + state tax owed, withholding gap, net shares
- Visualization of what you actually receive vs grant
- Multi-year vesting schedule projection

**Why it's needed:**
Many employees are surprised by "supplemental income" withholding rates and owe money at tax time. This tool prevents surprises.

---

### 2. ISO vs NSO Decision Tool

**Problem:** ISOs and NSOs have fundamentally different tax treatment, but employees don't know which is better for their situation.

**Features:**
- Input: Grant details, income, holding period plans
- Compare tax outcomes for both types
- AMT impact modeling for ISOs
- Optimal holding period recommendations
- Early exercise + 83(b) scenario

**Key insight:** ISOs aren't always better despite "favorable" treatment. AMT can make NSOs superior in some cases.

---

### 3. Stock Option Exercise Optimizer

**Problem:** When and how much to exercise is one of the most consequential financial decisions, yet most employees guess.

**Features:**
- Input: Options details, FMV, personal finances
- Model different exercise timing scenarios
- AMT impact calculation
- Cash flow requirements
- Compare: exercise now vs wait
- 83(b) election analysis

**Key scenarios:**
- Early exercise before FMV increases
- Exercise and hold for LTCG
- Exercise and sell immediately
- Partial exercise strategies

---

### 4. 83(b) Election Analyzer

**Problem:** The 83(b) election must be made within 30 days and is irrevocable. Wrong decision can cost huge money either way.

**Features:**
- Input: Stock type, FMV, expected growth, vesting schedule
- Calculate tax due if elect vs don't elect
- Model different growth scenarios
- Worst case analysis (company fails)
- Deadline countdown timer
- Filing instructions

**Critical insight:** 83(b) is often right for early employees with low FMV, but can be catastrophic if company fails.

---

### 5. Equity Concentration Risk Assessment

**Problem:** Tech employees often have 50-90% of net worth in employer stock, creating dangerous concentration risk.

**Features:**
- Input: Equity holdings, other assets, income sources
- Concentration percentage calculation
- Risk visualization (what if stock drops 50%?)
- Diversification recommendations
- Compare to "prudent" allocation
- Historical examples (Enron, Intel layoffs)

**Educational component:** Explain why concentration risk is "uncompensated" riskâ€”you're not rewarded for taking it.

---

### 6. Total Compensation Calculator

**Problem:** Comparing job offers with different equity structures is nearly impossible without tools.

**Features:**
- Input: Base, bonus, equity grants, vesting schedules
- Annualized total comp calculation
- Account for different vesting cliffs/schedules
- Refresh grant assumptions
- Company stage risk adjustment
- HCOL vs LCOL comparison

**Inspired by:** FAANG FIRE's Total Compensation Dashboard

---

### 7. ESPP Optimizer

**Problem:** ESPPs are often "free money" but employees don't understand optimal contribution strategies.

**Features:**
- Input: ESPP terms (discount, lookback), income
- Calculate guaranteed return
- Optimal contribution amount
- Cash flow planning
- Sell immediately vs hold decision
- Compare to other investment options

**Key insight:** ESPPs with lookback provision can provide 15-30%+ guaranteed returns. Not maxing is leaving money on table.

---

### 8. Post-Termination Exercise Planner

**Problem:** When leaving a company, employees have 90 days to exercise ISOs or lose them. This creates urgent, high-stakes decisions.

**Features:**
- Countdown timer (90-day deadline)
- Exercise cost calculation (strike + taxes)
- Funding options comparison
- Partial exercise scenarios
- "Can I afford to exercise?" assessment
- What happens if you don't exercise

**Critical:** Many employees let valuable options expire because they can't afford exercise + AMT.

---

### 9. IPO/Liquidity Event Planner

**Problem:** Liquidity events create sudden, complex tax situations that employees are unprepared for.

**Features:**
- Input: Holdings, cost basis, expected price
- Model tax scenarios at different prices
- Lock-up period planning
- Diversification schedule post-lockup
- QSBS exclusion calculator
- Capital gains vs ordinary income breakdown

**Key strategies:**
- 10b5-1 plan timing
- Charitable giving of appreciated shares
- Tax-loss harvesting opportunities
- State tax planning (move before liquidity?)

---

### 10. QSBS (Qualified Small Business Stock) Checker

**Problem:** QSBS can exclude up to $10-15M in capital gains, but employees don't know if they qualify.

**Features:**
- Eligibility checker (company requirements)
- Holding period tracker
- Exclusion amount calculator
- State conformity check
- Stacking strategies (trusts)
- Section 1045 rollover guidance

**2025 update:** One Big Beautiful Bill Act increased limits to $15M and introduced tiered exclusions.
