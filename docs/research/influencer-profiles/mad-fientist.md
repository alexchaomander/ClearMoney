## Key Philosophy

### Tax Avoidance is Legal and Powerful
The key insight: tax planning for early retirees is fundamentally different from traditional retirement planning. Most financial advice is wrong for FIRE seekers.

### The Guinea Pig Experiment
Brandon ran a detailed simulation comparing an "Optimized" investor using his strategies vs a "Normal" investor. Result: The optimized version had $100,000+ more and could retire almost 2 years earlier—with identical income and spending.

## Core Strategies

### 1. Traditional IRA > Roth IRA (for Early Retirees)
Counterintuitively, Traditional IRAs beat Roth IRAs for most early retirees because:
- You avoid taxes during high-earning years (potentially 24-37% brackets)
- You convert to Roth during early retirement at 0-12% brackets
- You end up with the benefits of BOTH

### 2. The Roth Conversion Ladder
The signature Mad Fientist strategy:
1. During working years: Maximize pre-tax contributions (401k, Traditional IRA)
2. Retire early with taxable brokerage account for bridge
3. Each year, convert Traditional IRA to Roth
4. Pay ~0-12% tax instead of the 24%+ you would have paid while working
5. After 5 years, the converted money is accessible penalty-free

### 3. HSA Triple Tax Advantage
The Health Savings Account provides:
- Tax deduction on contributions
- Tax-free growth
- Tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses

But the hack: Pay medical expenses out of pocket, let HSA grow tax-free for decades, then reimburse yourself tax-free in retirement.

### 4. Front-Loading Tax-Advantaged Accounts
Max out retirement accounts at the beginning of the year, not spread throughout. Benefits:
- More time in market (statistically advantageous)
- Tax savings have more time to compound

### 5. Accessing Retirement Funds Early
Multiple methods to access retirement accounts before 59.5:
- **Roth Conversion Ladder:** Convert, wait 5 years, access
- **Rule of 55:** Retire after 55, access that employer's 401k
- **457 Plans:** No early withdrawal penalty
- **72(t)/SEPP:** Substantially Equal Periodic Payments
- **HSA Reimbursement:** Reimburse past medical expenses
